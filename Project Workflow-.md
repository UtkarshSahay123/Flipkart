Our solution is an AI-powered Event-Driven Traffic Management System that helps traffic authorities predict and manage congestion before it becomes critical. The platform consists of a web-based dashboard with an interactive city map displaying live traffic events, congestion hotspots, road conditions, and citizen-reported incidents such as potholes, accidents, waterlogging, or broken traffic signals. Instead of simply reacting to incidents, the system uses historical traffic data, real-time camera feeds, and event information (such as political rallies, festivals, sports events, construction activities, and sudden gatherings) to forecast congestion levels, estimate affected areas, and predict the expected duration of traffic disruption. Computer vision models analyze traffic camera feeds to perform vehicle counting and traffic density estimation, while citizen-uploaded images are automatically classified using AI to verify road issues before updating the dashboard. Based on the predicted traffic impact, a recommendation engine suggests the optimal deployment of traffic police, barricade placement, emergency vehicles, and diversion routes using graph algorithms like Dijkstra for route optimization. The dashboard also features a "Traffic Digital Twin," providing a real-time and future visualization of congestion zones, recommended diversion paths, and resource deployment, enabling authorities to make faster and more informed decisions. Additionally, an AI assistant allows traffic officers to query specific locations or upcoming events and instantly receive actionable recommendations, making the system a complete decision-support platform rather than just a traffic monitoring website.
